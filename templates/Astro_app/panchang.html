What is Panchang?

Panchang is a Hindu Vedic calendar used to determine important dates and times for religious and cultural events. It is based on astronomical calculations and consists of five key elements:

  • Tithi (Lunar Day): The phase of the moon.
  • Vara (Day of the Week): The seven days of the week.
  • Nakshatra (Constellation): The star in which the moon is positioned.
  • Yoga (Lunar Union): The combination of Sun and Moon positions.
  • Karana (Half Tithi): A half-day based on Tithi divisions.

Panchang is essential in determining auspicious and inauspicious timings for rituals, festivals, and daily activities.

Five Elements of Panchang

1. Tithi (तिथि) - Lunar Day

Tithi is the time taken by the Moon to move 12 degrees ahead of the Sun. There are 30 Tithis in a lunar month, divided into two phases:

Tithi Name Phase Importance
Pratipada Both Beginning of festivals
Dwitiya Both Bhai Dooj, important vrat
Tritiya Both Akshaya Tritiya (wealth & success)
Chaturthi Both Sankashti Chaturthi (Lord Ganesha)
Panchami Both Nag Panchami (serpent worship)
Shashthi Both Skanda Shashti (Lord Kartikeya)
Saptami Both Ratha Saptami (Sun worship)
Ashtami Both Krishna Janmashtami, Durga Ashtami
Navami Both Ram Navami, Navratri
Dashami Both Vijaya Dashami (Dussehra)
Ekadashi Both Ekadashi fasting
Dwadashi Both Mokshada Dwadashi
Trayodashi Both Pradosh Vrat, Maha Shivratri
Chaturdashi Both Naraka Chaturdashi
Purnima Shukla Full Moon (Purnima) – important for fasting & rituals
Amavasya Krishna No Moon (Amavasya) – Pitru Tarpan, ancestor prayers

2. Vara (वार) - Days of the Week

The seven days are ruled by different planets:

Day Planet Significance
Sunday (Raviwar) Sun (Surya) Health & Leadership
Monday (Somwar) Moon (Chandra) Emotions & Mind
Tuesday (Mangalwar) Mars (Mangal) Strength & Energy
Wednesday (Budhwar) Mercury (Budh) Intelligence & Communication
Thursday (Guruvar) Jupiter (Guru) Knowledge & Prosperity
Friday (Shukrawar) Venus (Shukra) Love & Luxury
Saturday (Shanivar) Saturn (Shani) Discipline & Karma

3. Nakshatra (नक्षत्र) - Constellations

Nakshatra refers to the 27 divisions of the zodiac, each representing a lunar mansion. Some important ones:

Nakshatra Significance
Ashwini Healing & speed
Rohini Creativity & prosperity
Mrigashira Curiosity & travel
Magha Royalty & ancestry
Hasta Skill & craftsmanship
Swati Independence & air element
Anuradha Friendship & devotion
Mula Transformation & spirituality

4. Yoga (योग) - Planetary Combination

There are 27 Yogas, formed by the Sun-Moon positions. Some important ones:

Yoga Significance
Vishkumbha Success & growth
Priti Happiness & charm
Shiva Auspicious for spiritual activities
Siddha Best for achieving goals
Vyatipata Considered inauspicious

5. Karana (करण) - Half Tithi

A Tithi is divided into two Karanas. There are 11 Karanas, classified into:

Type Karanas Significance
Movable Karanas Bava, Balava, Kaulava, Taitila, Garaja, Vanija Good for starting work
Fixed Karanas Shakuni, Chatushpada, Nagava, Kimstughna Good for rituals

Auspicious & Inauspicious Timings in Panchang

Shubh Muhurat (Auspicious Timings)

  • Abhijit Muhurat: Best time for important activities (near midday).
  • Brahma Muhurat: Ideal for meditation and prayers (4:00 AM - 5:30 AM).
  • Vijaya Muhurat: Best for victories and success.

Inauspicious Timings (Avoid for Major Work)

  • Rahu Kaal (राहु काल): Time of Rahu, considered inauspicious for new beginnings.
  • Gulika Kaal: Not favorable for financial matters.
  • Yamaganda Kaal: Avoid important travel or decisions.
